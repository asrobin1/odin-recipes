Stirfry

Introduction

Nothing makes me feel like a 5 star chef than the simplistic yet majestic art of crafting a stirfry. Okay maybe not a super chef, but I certainly enjoy the ease and taste of a good stirfry. What I love most is how much flavor it brings to the vegetables and protein. You can tailor a stirfry to whatever dietary needs you have. Just remember to cook each ingredient separately with your leafy greens and noodles dead last!

Ingredients

  • Protein: chicken, tofu, beef, etc.
  • Vegetables: I prefer mushrooms, cole slaw, and spinach, but I've had great results with zucchini, squash, and snow peas!
  • Sauce: 3 parts low sodium soy sauce, 1 part sesame oil, 1 part rice vinegar, 1 part oyster sauce, 1 part hoisin sauce. I recommend a dash of Worchester sauce and a little ginger.
  • Green onion for garnish at the end.
  • Noodles of your choice.

Steps

  1. Prep all ingredients: boil noodles, mix sauce, cut veggies and protein.
  2. Grease a wok and cook protein on medium-high heat until light golden brown. Separate your protein for later.
  3. Rinse and repeat with your vegetables. NOTE: Do NOT cook your green onion!
  4. Turn your stove to medium-low heat and combine your protein, vegetables, and noodles. Pour in your sauce and mix thoroughly.
  5. Turn off your stove and garnish your dish with green onion. Serve up your plate, and enjoy your meal.
